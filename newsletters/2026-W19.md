## 📣 Top Stories

### 1. Rakuten × Impact.com — The Deal That Ends the Affiliate Platform Era

The affiliate industry's two longest-running platform rivals just made fragmentation the industry's problem, not theirs. On Monday, **Rakuten International and impact.com announced a strategic alliance** that amounts to Rakuten exiting the affiliate technology platform business entirely. Thousands of merchants and publishers on Rakuten's dashboard will migrate to impact.com's infrastructure. Rakuten keeps what it actually owns: **Rakuten Rewards (20M+ US members)**, its managed-services agency, and its first-party data. Impact gets a flood of new publisher supply and a "Titanium Partner" designation for Rakuten inside its own marketplace. No equity changed hands. The practical effect is consolidation without acquisition.

Impact CEO David Yovanno framed it in the clearest terms: the affiliate channel has finally "put on the muscle" to compete with Google, Amazon, and Meta for always-on performance budgets. The deal is a structural bet that fewer, larger platforms outperform the historically fragmented network landscape. With US affiliate spend at **$13.81 billion** and driving **$241 billion in ecommerce sales** in 2026, the bet is not without basis.

**What it means for vendors:** Three immediate actions. First, if you run affiliate programs on Rakuten Advertising, migration conversations will begin as early as this summer — document your tracking pixels, publisher lists, and API integrations *now*. Second, Impact is gaining enormous leverage as the dominant platform; lock in terms before the consolidation premium hits. Third, Rakuten Rewards is now a pure publisher — the largest cashback/loyalty network in the US operating on impact.com's rails. That changes every merchant's negotiation posture with Rakuten from "platform partner" to "publisher you buy against."

### 2. The Agentic Commerce Infrastructure Stack Just Completed

Last week, three things happened in parallel that together solve the last major barrier to AI agents operating as trackable affiliate partners. Collectively, they signal that the channel we've been calling "agentic commerce" is no longer theoretical infrastructure — it's live rails.

**Experian launched Agent Trust** — a framework for creating verified identity bindings between human consumers and the AI agents acting for them. Visa, Cloudflare, and Skyfire are collaborators. The system issues real-time trust tokens linking a human's identity to an agent's transaction intent: **"Know Your Agent,"** modeled explicitly on Know Your Customer financial compliance. When an agent carries a verified trust token, the merchant knows the transaction is authorized. More critically for affiliates: the agent becomes a traceable, commissionable entity.

**Visa launched Intelligent Commerce**, a formal initiative for AI agents to transact on behalf of consumers. Combined with Experian's Agent Trust, the payment rails and identity layer for agentic purchases now both exist and are live in production.

**The A2A (Agent-to-Agent) protocol crossed 150+ production deployments**, now running across Microsoft Azure AI Foundry, Amazon Bedrock AgentCore, and Salesforce. A2A is the communication standard that lets shopping agents negotiate directly with merchant agents — without a browser, without a cookie, without a human in the loop. It just crossed from pilot to production scale.

Stack that against Amazon's Q1 earnings confirmation that Rufus has grown monthly users **115% and engagement 400% year-over-year**, and Shopify's report of **8x AI-driven traffic** and **15x AI-driven orders** since January 2025. The channel exists. The identity layer exists. The payment infrastructure exists. The communication protocol exists. The only thing not yet solved is affiliate attribution inside these agent flows — and that's where the next three months of competitive advantage will be built.

### 3. Meta Cements Native Affiliate Infrastructure Across Instagram and Facebook

Meta made it formal this week: Instagram Reels are natively shoppable with affiliate links. Creators tag up to 30 products per Reel, attributed commerce flows through Meta's Partnership Ads Hub, and Meta CEO Nicola Mendelsohn declared "the era of link-in-bio is finally over." On Facebook, **eBay joined Amazon as a launch partner** in the Facebook Affiliate Partnerships beta, with Shopee live across eight markets in Southeast Asia and Latin America.

This is significant for two reasons. First, the network effect: Meta's combined platform reach (Facebook + Instagram) dwarfs every other social commerce surface. When that surface turns on performance-based affiliate attribution natively, it doesn't marginally improve social commerce — it reconfigures it. Second, the closed-loop problem: Meta now controls discovery (News Feed/Reels), purchase intent (product tags), conversion (in-app checkout), and attribution (first-party). Brands whose product catalogs aren't enrolled in Meta Commerce are structurally invisible on the most distributed affiliate surface ever built.

**For Anker:** The eBay expansion creates a direct pathway. Anker's significant eBay inventory — new + refurbished across all major product lines — can now surface as affiliate-tagged content in Facebook posts and Reels without requiring Meta Commerce Manager enrollment. eBay handles the infrastructure. The only required action is confirming eBay listing optimization. Low-effort, incremental reach at no additional commission cost.

### 4. GRIN Disrupts Affiliate Platform Economics: Free Infrastructure, AI Monetization

GRIN announced this week that its full affiliate infrastructure is free — no platform fees, no revenue share on commissions. Brands can launch a complete affiliate program in minutes at zero upfront cost. The monetization model shifts entirely to **Gia**, GRIN's AI agent that handles partner discovery, outreach, and optimization.

This is the creator-economy pricing playbook applied to traditional affiliate: when the platform's revenue depends on AI services rather than SaaS subscriptions, the infrastructure commodity becomes a customer acquisition vehicle. The signal for incumbents (Impact, CJ, Awin) is clear: platform fees are under structural pressure. The signal for vendors: the barrier to launching creator-affiliate programs just dropped to near-zero, which means your competitors have no pricing excuse not to be in the channel.

**Practical implication:** GRIN's free tier is best positioned as a top-of-funnel partner discovery tool, particularly for nano/micro creators (10K–100K followers) where the management overhead historically exceeded the ROI. Use it to identify and test creator partnerships, but maintain your own attribution tracking — a platform monetized through AI optimization has incentives that may not perfectly align with your program quality goals.

### 5. IAB Australia + eMarketer Put Numbers on What We Already Knew

Two research releases this week closed the loop on the affiliate growth argument. IAB Australia's **2026 State of the Nation** report confirmed: 42% of advertisers increased affiliate investment this year, two-thirds of publishers reported revenue growth, and 90% expect affiliate's importance to grow further. **Travel has overtaken retail as the top-performing publisher vertical.** eMarketer's definitive 2026 affiliate FAQ landed the same week, projecting **$241 billion in US affiliate-driven ecommerce** and identifying AI chatbots and creators as the two primary forces reshaping the channel. Global affiliate spend has nearly doubled from $9.6 billion in 2020 to $19.4 billion this year.

**The underappreciated finding:** eMarketer's report calls out a measurement gap — current attribution frameworks still can't capture affiliate's full contribution to the purchase journey. For vendors: this is a competitive arbitrage opportunity. Brands that invest in better multi-touch attribution *now* will demonstrate ROI to internal budget holders that peers running last-click models cannot. The channel is growing into a visibility gap. Own the attribution.

**UGREEN's Commission Ceiling Still Outflanks Anker**

With UGREEN expanding aggressively into NAS and home networking in 2026 — creating new content hooks for the same tier-1 publishers that cover Anker — the 7-point commission ceiling gap is now a retention risk, not just a recruitment gap. **Aventon Remains Priority's Strongest DTC Competitor**

Priority's above-average commission and exceptional editorial partner quality are genuine competitive advantages — they're just not being fully monetized because the attribution infrastructure (AvantLink leakage, no network expansion) isn't capturing all the referrals those advantages generate.

**Fintech Affiliate Vertical Underinvested Across the Board**

For Anker (consumer electronics, installment financing adjacent) and Priority (high-ticket e-bikes, BNPL natural fit), this is a category with minimal competition from brand-side investment and high publisher commission expectations. Early movers in fintech affiliate partnerships in Q2 face limited competition.
