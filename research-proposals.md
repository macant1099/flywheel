## PROP-001 · FTC 2026 Compliance: Disclosure Rules Expanded to Livestreams, Short-Form Video & AI Endorsements

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | proposed |
| **Category** | industry/regulation-compliance |
| **Surfaced** | 2026-04-13 |

**Signal sources:**
- Tapfiliate (Feb 12 2026): "FTC's 2026 updates expand disclosure rules to cover new formats like live streams and short-form video. California has strengthened privacy rights, while Texas introduced social media transparency rules." — https://tapfiliate.com/blog/affiliate-marketing-compliance-gp/
- Affiverse (Mar 2026): "In January 2026, the FTC issued its first warning letters under the Consumer Review Rule, citing ten companies for potential violations including the use of fake reviews, incentivized testimonials, and deceptive review practices." — https://www.affiversemedia.com/the-ftc-is-watching-ai-generated-endorsements-affiliate-links-and-what-compliance-looks-like-in-2026/
- Automateed (Mar 2026): "In 2026, the FTC has increased enforcement, especially targeting micro-influencers and AI/virtual endorsers." — https://www.automateed.com/ftc-disclosure-rules-for-affiliates

**Why it matters:**
LiveChannel is a livestream commerce platform. The FTC's 2026 expansion of disclosure rules to cover livestreams and short-form video directly affects every vendor and creator operating on LiveChannel. Non-compliance risk is material: January 2026 saw the first enforcement letters. Additionally, the extension of rules to AI-generated endorsements creates new liability surface as vendors begin using AI tools to produce affiliate content. This is structurally distinct from tracking-privacy.md (which covers technical attribution gaps) — this is legal exposure and compliance posture for vendors.

- YepAds (Apr 2026): Comprehensive tool guide including Sora (video), Flux.1 (image), and content creation stack for affiliate marketers — https://yepads.com/affiliate-marketers-will-use-these-ai-tools-in-2025/

**Why it matters:**
The existing `industry/ai-agent-affiliates.md` covers AI *as buyer and affiliate* — AI agents making purchases on behalf of consumers. The implications for LiveChannel vendors are material: (1) their affiliate partners can now produce 10x more content with AI tools, changing quality-control and brand-safety dynamics; (2) AI-generated affiliate content carries FTC disclosure risk (intersecting with PROP-001's compliance angle); (3) the barrier to entry for affiliate content production has collapsed, expanding the publisher pool but also inflating low-quality content volume; (4) understanding which AI tools affiliates are using helps vendors design program materials and briefs that are AI-tool-friendly (structured specs, image assets, demo video hooks). This is the affiliate program operations angle of the AI transformation — not covered anywhere in the current research library.

## PROP-003 · Membership Commerce + Recurring Affiliate Commission Models

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | proposed |
| **Category** | industry/models |
| **Surfaced** | 2026-04-13 |

**Signal sources:**
- ReferralCandy (Feb 10 2026): "Membership Program Ecommerce: The Complete 2026 Guide to Building Recurring Revenue" — https://www.referralcandy.com/blog/membership-program-ecommerce-the-complete-2026-guide-to-building-recurring-revenue
- TechRepublic (Jan 30 2026): Subscription commerce as a structured ecommerce model, recurring billing cycles and recurring affiliate payouts — https://www.techrepublic.com/article/subscription-commerce-guide/
- DigitalApplied (Feb 21 2026): "Subscription Commerce 2026: Recurring Revenue Guide" — pricing models and retention strategies — https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/subscription-commerce-2026-recurring-revenue-guide
- Jotform / Taggbox (Mar-Apr 2026): Multiple programs highlighting 20–30% recurring commissions as affiliate recruitment advantage for subscription-based brands

**Why it matters:**
As DTC brands increasingly shift toward membership tiers and subscription replenishment models (think coffee, supplements, outdoor consumables), the affiliate commission structure changes fundamentally: from one-time CPA to recurring monthly payouts. This creates higher lifetime value for affiliates and stronger loyalty incentives. For LiveChannel vendors in verticals like outdoor gear, consumer goods, and health products, understanding how to structure membership-linked affiliate programs is a competitive differentiator. Structurally distinct from all existing research files.
