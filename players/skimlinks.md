## Overview

Skimlinks is not a network and not a platform in the traditional sense — it is the **publisher monetization layer** that sits between editorial content and the affiliate ecosystem. Publishers (media companies, blogs, review sites) install Skimlinks' JavaScript snippet; from that point on, every product link in their content is automatically converted into a tracked affiliate link connected to 48,500+ merchant programs, without the publisher needing to individually join each network.

The company's position in the ecosystem is unique and powerful: Skimlinks operates as an aggregator of affiliate relationships on behalf of publishers, meaning it has significant volume-based negotiating leverage with networks and merchants. For vendors, Skimlinks is how major editorial publishers like Condé Nast, Hearst, BuzzFeed, and thousands of independent blogs monetize their product mentions without maintaining individual affiliate relationships.

Being "accessible via Skimlinks" is therefore a meaningful publisher distribution lever for vendors — it means your merchant program can be automatically monetized by 60,000 publishers who have never proactively chosen to work with you.

## How It Works

**For Publishers:**
1. Install Skimlinks JavaScript snippet on their website
2. Any product URL in content (editorial, reviews, listicles) is automatically converted to a tracked affiliate link
3. Skimlinks routes through the affiliate network that pays the best rate for that merchant
4. Publisher earns commissions consolidated through Skimlinks' single payout system
5. No need to individually join each affiliate network or apply to each merchant program

**For Vendors (Merchants):**
1. Run an affiliate program on any major network (CJ, Awin, ShareASale, Rakuten, etc.)
2. Skimlinks automatically picks up your program and makes it accessible to its publisher base
3. Commission rates, cookie windows, and terms flow through from the underlying network
4. Vendors can "prefer" Skimlinks to prioritize it or upgrade to "Preferred Partner" status for premium placement

**The aggregation model** means Skimlinks publishers may be linking to your products without you explicitly recruiting them — it's passive affiliate distribution that scales with editorial content volume.

## Key Products & Services

**Skimlinks Core** — The JavaScript snippet that auto-monetizes any publisher's outbound product links across 48,500+ programs.

**SkimWords** — Contextual text-to-affiliate conversion that automatically makes product keywords in text clickable with affiliate tracking.

**Skimlinks Merchant Hub** — Dashboard for merchants to view Skimlinks-driven traffic, optimize commission rates, and manage preferred publisher relationships.

**Preferred Partner Program** — Elevated program for merchants wanting premium placement, editorial recommendations, and dedicated publisher development attention within Skimlinks' network.

**Commerce Content Analytics** — Publisher-side performance reporting showing which editorial content, product categories, and individual links drive the most affiliate revenue — helping publishers optimize future content for commerce.

**Audience Insights** — Aggregated behavioral data from the publisher network, available to participating merchants for audience intelligence.

## Pricing / Commission Model

**For Publishers:**
- Free to join
- Skimlinks takes a **25% revenue share** of all affiliate commissions generated through the platform
- Publishers keep 75% of commissions earned

**For Merchants:**
- No separate Skimlinks fee beyond what's paid to the underlying affiliate network
- Preferred Partner programs have a premium cost (custom pricing)
- The underlying network transaction override still applies (e.g., CJ's ~25% override)

This stacking of fees (network override + Skimlinks revenue share) means vendors are paying multiple layers of intermediary costs — important to factor into CPA modeling.

## Strengths

- **Editorial publisher reach**: Skimlinks is how 60,000 publishers — including major media companies — monetize product content. Being accessible via Skimlinks gives passive distribution to publishers who'd never proactively join your program
- **Taboola integration**: As a Taboola subsidiary, Skimlinks has access to massive content distribution infrastructure and editorial publisher relationships across the open web
- **Publisher quality**: Skimlinks skews toward editorial content (journalism, reviews, lifestyle) rather than coupon/cashback — higher brand equity affiliate traffic
- **Cross-network arbitrage**: Skimlinks routes through whichever network pays best for a given merchant, meaning publishers always earn optimally — which incentivizes them to keep Skimlinks installed

## Weaknesses

- **Fee stacking**: 25% Skimlinks share + underlying network override means vendor effective affiliate costs are notably higher than direct program management
- **Passive distribution**: Vendors can't directly target Skimlinks publishers; the relationship is mediated through editorial content that may or may not mention your products
- **Attribution complexity**: Commission attribution through Skimlinks flows through underlying networks, creating potential last-click conflicts with direct affiliate relationships
- **Not a replacement for direct programs**: Skimlinks is a supplement, not a strategy — it handles passive monetization of product mentions but doesn't build publisher relationships

## Vendor Takeaway

Skimlinks is **passive affiliate distribution infrastructure** — a background channel that ensures editorial publishers automatically earn commissions when they mention your products. For LiveChannel vendors, the action item is simple: run programs on the major networks (CJ, Awin, ShareASale) that Skimlinks aggregates, and consider upgrading to Preferred Partner status if editorial commerce is a meaningful traffic source for your category.

**Best fit for:** Consumer goods in beauty, fashion, home, tech, and wellness categories where editorial "best of" and review content drives purchase intent.

**ROI reality check:** Calculate your true cost per affiliate sale including network override + Skimlinks share. For some categories, the editorial quality justifies the cost; for others, direct publisher development on impact.com may be more efficient.
