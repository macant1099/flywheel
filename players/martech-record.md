## Overview

Martech Record is an independent trade publication covering the intersection of marketing, content, and commerce, with a concentrated focus on affiliate marketing, partnership marketing, and commerce technology. Founded and operated by Michael McNerney, the publication serves as one of the primary editorial voices for senior affiliate and partnership marketing professionals in the US market.

Unlike broader marketing trade publications (AdExchanger, Adweek, Digiday), Martech Record is laser-focused on affiliate and partner marketing — covering platform updates, industry strategy, regulatory developments, and the emerging intersection of AI-driven discovery with affiliate commerce. Its independence from any network or platform owner gives it credibility across the ecosystem.

Martech Record also produces the MARCC (Marketing, Content & Commerce) conference series, which takes place in Los Angeles, Denver, and New York City, serving as an in-person gathering point for senior affiliate and partnership marketing professionals.

## What It Does

### Editorial (martechrecord.com)
Daily-to-weekly trade editorial covering:
- Affiliate and partnership platform news (Impact.com, CJ, Awin, Rakuten, Partnerize)
- Publisher strategy and commerce content monetization
- AI disruption of search, discovery, and affiliate attribution
- Industry research and data reports
- Executive interviews and analysis
- Regulatory and compliance developments

### MARCC Conference Series
Martech Record produces the Marketing, Content & Commerce (MARCC) conference series, which in 2026 operates across three markets:
- **MARCC LA** — Los Angeles flagship event, typically held in Q1/Q2
- **MARCC Denver** — Mid-year event
- **MARCC NYC / East Coast** — Q4 event

These events bring together brand-side affiliate managers, publishers, networks, platforms, and agencies for sessions focused on affiliate strategy, AI's impact on commerce, and publisher monetization.

### Demo Days
A virtual events series where affiliate and partner marketing technology platforms demonstrate new features and innovations to the trade community. Run quarterly, Demo Days serves as a discovery and evaluation platform for brands and agencies scouting affiliate technology.

### Research Reports
Martech Record publishes research reports in partnership with industry stakeholders, covering affiliate spend trends, publisher performance, and technology adoption.

## Coverage Focus Areas (2026)

Key editorial themes in 2026:

- **AI and Affiliate Attribution**: How AI shopping agents (Perplexity, OpenAI, Google AI Overviews) are changing the affiliate attribution model and what publishers/brands should do about it
- **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)**: Coverage of Google's proposed open standard for product data exchange between brands and AI agents — a potential game-changer for affiliate tracking
- **Creator Commerce**: The convergence of influencer marketing with performance-based affiliate commerce
- **Platform Consolidation**: Deep coverage of the Rakuten/Impact.com alliance, Awin/ShareASale merger, and other structural shifts
- **Publisher Strategy**: How editorial publishers are adapting to AI search disruption through affiliate revenue diversification

## Key Differentiators

- **Deep Specialization**: Unlike general martech media, Martech Record goes deep on affiliate and partner marketing — the editorial equivalent of a vertical trade pub
- **Independent Ownership**: Not owned by a network, platform, or holding company, preserving editorial credibility
- **Practitioner Audience**: Readership skews toward mid-to-senior-level practitioners actually running affiliate programs, not just executives
- **Event Integration**: The MARCC series connects the editorial brand to in-person community building
- **US Market Leadership**: Strongest voice in US-focused affiliate and partner marketing trade media

## Strengths

- **Credibility**: Independence and specialization earn trust from practitioners across the ecosystem
- **Event Franchise**: MARCC conferences create community, revenue, and editorial opportunities beyond the website
- **Coverage Quality**: Detailed, analytical coverage goes beyond press release aggregation
- **Timeliness**: Consistent coverage of breaking industry news (Rakuten/Impact alliance, ShareASale migration, etc.)
- **Google UCP Coverage**: First major trade outlet to cover Google's Universal Commerce Protocol implications for affiliate

## Weaknesses

- **Scale**: Smaller audience than general marketing trade publications
- **US Focus**: Less coverage of UK/European affiliate market developments compared to Affiverse
- **Subscription Model**: Some deeper content behind membership wall limits reach
- **Resource Constraints**: Smaller editorial team compared to larger trade publications
