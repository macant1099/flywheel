# Flywheel Affiliate Daily

## The Lead

**Rakuten and Impact.com Join Forces — and the Affiliate Stack Just Got Simpler**

Two days ago, Rakuten International and Impact.com announced a strategic alliance that effectively merges their go-to-market in affiliate and partnership marketing. The deal is not an acquisition — there's no equity exchange — but the operational impact is enormous. Rakuten is exiting the platform technology business. Thousands of merchants and publishers on Rakuten's tracking and dashboard infrastructure will migrate to Impact.com's system, while Rakuten doubles down on what it actually owns: Rakuten Rewards, its managed-service agency business, and its first-party consumer data.

For vendors, this is a simplification event. Instead of managing programs across two competing networks with separate dashboards, reporting schemas, and publisher pools, the industry's two oldest affiliate platforms will now operate on shared rails. Impact.com gets Rakuten's massive publisher footprint (Rakuten becomes a "Titanium Partner" inside the Impact platform), and Rakuten gets to stop spending on commodity SaaS infrastructure it no longer wants to differentiate on.

The strategic subtext is more interesting than the press release. Impact CEO David Yovanno framed the move as affiliate marketing finally putting on enough muscle to compete with Google, Amazon, and Meta for always-on performance budgets. That's ambitious, but not unfounded — EMARKETER estimates affiliate will drive $241 billion in US ecommerce sales this year. The channel's problem was never scale; it was fragmentation. This alliance is a direct response.

What should vendors watch for? Migration timelines, publisher overlap, and whether the combined entity can deliver on the promise of unified measurement across social links, creator content, and traditional affiliate. If you're on Rakuten's platform today, expect a transition roadmap in the coming weeks. If you're on Impact, expect a lot of new publisher inventory showing up in your marketplace.

- **Instagram relaunched its creator affiliate commerce tools.** The new system is an open affiliate link model, distinct from Facebook's closed partner approach. Commission pathways, attribution windows, and enrollment rules differ between the two — don't assume they're interchangeable.

- **EMARKETER pegs 2026 US affiliate-driven ecommerce at $241 billion.** The channel is at an inflection point where AI chatbots and creators are reshaping discovery, but measurement frameworks still can't capture affiliate's full contribution.

- **Shopify merchants are now discoverable inside ChatGPT, Copilot, Google AI Mode, and Gemini.** Agentic commerce just got a native Shopify integration layer. If you're on Shopify, your products can now surface in AI conversations without additional affiliate setup.

## Partner Landscape

**Profile: Loyalty and Cashback Programs**

The Rakuten/Impact.com alliance puts a spotlight on loyalty and cashback as an affiliate partner category. Rakuten Rewards remains one of the largest cashback publishers in the world, and its decision to shed technology in favor of doubling down on consumer-facing rewards signals where the smart money is heading.

Loyalty programs convert at rates traditional content affiliates envy — consumers arrive pre-motivated to purchase because they're chasing a reward. The trade-off is margin: cashback requires funding the rebate, which means your effective commission cost is higher. But the incrementality argument is strong. Loyalty traffic tends to convert customers who were already in-market, shortening the attribution path and reducing wasted spend on upper-funnel impressions that never close.

For vendors evaluating loyalty partners, the question isn't whether to participate — it's how to structure it. Tiered cashback (higher rewards for first-time buyers, lower for repeat) can turn a margin-eroding channel into a customer acquisition engine. The Rakuten/Impact consolidation will likely make it easier to manage these relationships from a single dashboard in the months ahead.

## The AI Angle

Amazon's Q1 earnings call revealed that Rufus, its AI shopping assistant, has grown monthly active users by 115% and engagement by nearly 400% year-over-year. CEO Andy Jassy was blunt about third-party AI agents still getting "the pricing right or the product information right," positioning Amazon's first-party data as its moat. Meanwhile, Shopify has opened its merchant catalog to ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, and Google's Gemini — meaning AI shopping agents can now surface Shopify products natively. The affiliate industry needs to answer a pressing question: when an AI agent drives a sale, who gets the commission?

## One Thing to Do Today

**Audit your network footprint.** If you run programs on both Rakuten and Impact.com, log in to each dashboard and export your active publisher list. Compare them. The coming migration will merge these pools, and you'll want a baseline to measure whether the consolidation actually expands your reach or just deduplicates partners you already had.
