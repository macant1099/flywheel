# Source-Side Landscape: Research Brief

## Why this research exists

Flywheel's affiliate research to date has been vendor-facing: which vendors should run affiliate programs, how well they execute, which partners they should recruit. This brief opens a complementary track that flips the lens. A private affiliate network has two sides, and the source side — membership organizations, clubs, communities, associations — is the harder, more defensible, and under-served half.

Before we can score sources, match them to vendors, or write playbooks, we need an honest map of what these organizations are doing today. Most of them are doing *something* in the member-deals space, even if it's just a coupon thread in a newsletter. What tools are they on? What's working? What's broken? Where's the white space LiveChannel can actually fill?

The goal of this research track is to answer those questions and keep answering them as the landscape changes.

## Scope

**In scope**
- Platforms and SaaS products that serve membership orgs with perks, discounts, or affiliate commerce capabilities.
- Association management systems (AMS) with member-discount modules.
- Mainstream affiliate networks viewed from the source/publisher seat.
- Vertical benchmark programs that define the category (AAA, AARP, Costco, USAA, major car clubs).
- Community and creator monetization adjacencies that inform member-deal UX.
- The DIY long tail — how small orgs actually run deals today when they have no platform.

**Out of scope (for now)**
- Consumer-side cashback and coupon tools (Honey, Rakuten consumer, Capital One Shopping) except as competitive context.
- Vendor-side affiliate management tools already covered under `research/networks/`.
- Employer benefits administration outside of the perks/deals dimension.

## Research buckets

1. **Affinity / perks platforms.** White-labeled member-perks marketplaces. Closest competitive analog to LiveChannel's source-facing proposition.
   - _Initial targets:_ Abenity, Access Development, PerkSpot, BenefitHub, Working Advantage, Tickets at Work, Fond, Entertainment Book, Corporate Perks, My Deals.

2. **Association management systems.** AMS platforms with built-in or add-on member-discount features.
   - _Initial targets:_ YourMembership, MemberClicks, Wild Apricot, Personify, Higher Logic, Naylor, Impexium, Fonteva, Nimble AMS.

3. **Affiliate networks from the source/publisher seat.** Evaluate the onboarding and ongoing experience an org leader has if they sign up as a publisher rather than as a merchant.
   - _Initial targets:_ Impact, CJ Affiliate, Rakuten Advertising, ShareASale, Awin, Partnerize, Refersion (already tracked vendor-side; re-examine from source POV).

4. **Vertical benchmark programs.** The flagship member-deal programs that set expectations across the category.
   - _Initial targets:_ AAA discounts, AARP Rewards / AARP Member Benefits, Costco member pricing, USAA member marketplace, BMW CCA member benefits, Porsche Club of America, SCCA, Harley Owners Group, National Trust for Historic Preservation, alumni association models (Penn State WeAre, Harvard Alumni benefits).

5. **Community and creator monetization adjacencies.** Not direct competitors but shape what modern members-only commerce looks like.
   - _Initial targets:_ Mighty Networks, Circle, Patreon, Substack, Kajabi, Discord boosting/subscriptions, LTK (creator-side as a source pattern).

6. **DIY long tail.** Pattern-level research rather than entity-level. How do small car clubs, hobby groups, local chapters, faith communities, and neighborhood orgs currently surface deals to members? Newsletter coupons, forum threads, sponsored events, Facebook group pinned posts, manual PDFs.

## Entry template

Each platform or program entry should capture:

- **Name, URL, owner/parent company**
- **Target source type.** Employer, professional association, club, alumni org, consumer membership, community, creator.
- **Source count / member reach** (if published or estimable).
- **Pricing model.** Who pays whom — source, vendor, both, per-click, per-transaction, SaaS fee, revenue share.
- **Vendor onboarding model.** Open marketplace, curated, invite-only.
- **Exclusivity posture.** Public codes, gated offers, truly exclusive pricing.
- **Identity / auth model.** How a member proves they're a member to access the deal.
- **Data visibility for the source.** What analytics and attribution the org actually sees.
- **UX surface.** Where offers live — web portal, email, app, embedded in AMS, physical card.
- **Integration depth.** Standalone site vs. embedded in org's own properties.
- **Apparent strengths.**
- **Apparent weaknesses / gaps.**
- **Implication for LiveChannel.** Direct competitor, adjacent, inspiration, or non-threat.
- **Citations / source links.**
- **Last reviewed date.**

## Deliverables

- **Phase 1 (initial sweep).** One entry file per researched platform under `research/sources/entries/`. A rolling summary at `research/sources/landscape.md` that aggregates findings into a readable State-of-the-Landscape digest.
- **Phase 2 (gaps + opportunity).** A `gaps.md` document identifying underserved source types, unmet feature needs, and where LiveChannel's walled-garden + auth bridge is genuinely differentiated.
- **Phase 3 (benchmarks).** A `benchmarks.md` capturing what "good" looks like — conversion rates, activation rates, member satisfaction — drawn from any public data or case studies.

## Cadence

- **Initial sweep:** 2-3 weeks to populate entries across all six buckets.
- **Continuing research:** monthly refresh of the landscape digest; new-entity alerts as they appear in affiliate/association press; quarterly gap-and-opportunity update.
- **Signal capture:** material product launches, acquisitions, or repositionings in this space get logged as dated signals in the landscape digest, matching the signal pattern used in macbee's research files.

## Resolved scoping decisions (2026-04-20)

1. **Sweep shape: breadth first, then car-club deep-dive.** The initial sweep populates entries across all six buckets. Once the landscape map is in place, the first vertical report is a car-club deep-dive.
2. **Primary audience: LiveChannel sales pipeline.** The eventual public-facing report is written for membership-org decision makers — club presidents, association executive directors, alumni relations leads. Tone is practical and outcome-focused; goal is inbound interest in LiveChannel, not neutral industry analysis.
3. **Research mode: desk-based only for now.** All research from public sources — websites, press, case studies, public reviews, published pricing. No outbound contact, trials, or interviews at this stage. Revisit after the initial sweep completes.
